Hello and welcome to Pythonic Introduction to Algorithms! The purpose of this blog is teach me deeper algorithm magic and hopefully work as a useful resource for a larger audience.

A longer introduction

The spark behind this project was my horrible failure at the 2007 ICFP Programming Contest. In the final analysis, it turned out that I have a true treasure in my bookshelf -- the Cormen & al. Introduction to Algorithms (1st ed.). I have rarely opened it since the spring of my freshman year (i.e. 1999).

While the book does give you the pseudo-code, there is small conversion step that must be done. For example, the book uses 1-based arrays while Python uses 0-based. There are also several other features in the newer Python releases that allow more Pythonic implementations of the pseudo-code.

What about this blog, then?

This brings us finally to the raison d'etre of the blog. I can fill the gaps in my algorithm knowledge by actually going through the book and implementing as many algorithms as possible. You can benefit by not having to worry about off-by-ones, accidental O(N) → O(N²) transformations &c.

I hope to post an entry roughly once a week. I will also try to go through the chapters in order, but exceptions may occur. I will not include other material from the book than the algorithms themselves and their short descriptions -- I respect the work of the authors. If you need the proofs and the explanations and all the rest, buy the book. You can also check out the MIT OpenCourseWare page for the course.

As for the edition, I will use the first edition. I have no pressing need to buy the newer edition -- unless you'd like to donate me a copy… =)

That's it. I'll appreciate any feedback, especially if the algorithm implementations are WRONG or TOO SLOW. You can best reach me by email. My address can be found with the Python interpreter:
